Walking Access to Clinics in Chicago

5, 10, and 20 minute walking isochrones from clinics, built with OpenStreetMap data, QuickOSM, and the ORS Tools plugin.

5, 10, and 20 minute walking isochrones from clinics across Chicago.

What methods and steps were taken?

Clinic data was downloaded using the "amenity=clinic" tag along with building footprints from OpenStreetMap and QuickOSM. Since clinics were a mix of points and polygons, the polygons were converted to centroids and merged into the existing points, producing a single layer representing every clinic location. The building layer was then filtered down to residential buildings using select by expression, and those residential polygons were also converted into centroid points.

The ORS Tools plugin, which connects to the OpenRouteService API, was used to generate the isochrones shown on the map. Isochrones are polygons showing the area reachable by 5, 10, and 20 minutes on foot from each clinic. The Count Points in Polygon command was then used to calculate the exact number of residential points within each isochrone ring.

Findings

The number of residents within walking distance varies greatly by location. In central, denser areas such as The Loop, some clinics have over 500 residences within a 20 minute walk. In contrast, several clinics further from downtown had as few as 1 to 15 residences within a 5 minute walk, suggesting the surrounding population is far sparser near those clinics. This points to an uneven distribution of clinics across Chicago, similar to the uneven distribution seen with other amenities such as food banks or schools.

Why isochrones?

Isochrones are a stronger way to analyze this data than simple buffer distances, since they reflect real walking routes along the street network rather than a straight, unrealistic line. A buffer distance would have overestimated access by including buildings separated from a clinic by highways or other barriers. Paired with the Count Points in Polygon command, isochrones give a way to quantify population access to clinics numerically rather than just visually.
